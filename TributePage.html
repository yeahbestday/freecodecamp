Dr. Norman Bethune

The man who pioneered mobile blood transfusion units in wartime medicine

Here's a time line of Dr. Bethune's life:

  • 1899 - Norman Bethune is born in Ontario, Canada.
  • 1924 - Bethune begins studying medicine at the University of Toronto.
  • 1936 - Bethune participates in the Spanish Civil War as a medical worker for the International Brigades and receives a medal from the Spanish government.
  • 1938 - After returning to Canada, Bethune travels to China.
  • 1939 - In China, Bethune joins the Chinese Communist Party and starts working in medical services in Yan'an.
  • 1940 - The Second Sino-Japanese War breaks out, and Bethune becomes the head of the Health Department of the Eighth Route Army in China.
  • 1941 - During the war, Bethune establishes the Bethune International Peace Hospital in Yan'an, a crucial medical institution during the Chinese resistance.
  • 1942 - Bethune becomes the director of the Health Department of the Central Military Commission of the Communist Party of China.
  • 1943 - Leading medical teams, Bethune provides vital medical support in the fight against Japanese invaders.
  • 1945 - After the victory in the war, Bethune participates in peace negotiations between the Chinese Communist Party and the Nationalist Party.
  • 1947 - Recognized for his contributions to medical work and healthcare reform in China, Bethune is posthumously honored as a combat hero of the People's Liberation Army.
  • 1949 - The People's Republic of China is established, and Bethune becomes the Minister of Health in the new government.
  • 1950 - Bethune succumbs to a severe illness and passes away at the age of 51.
